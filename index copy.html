Services

Data is simply everywhere. However, the more existence of data does not create added value for your business. I help you to clear out your data cellar, bring color into play and focus the spotlight only on what is relevant.

The individual steps to a better data world can be found here:

The data cellar is dark and dusty. You constantly stumble across data lying around. In addition, there are many moments in your daily work where working with data requires a lot of manual effort. Together, we formulate your questions, work out your use cases, design solutions and point the flashlight on everything relevant.

The goal is deﬁned. Now it is particularly important to go through everything in detail and get a precise overview of the data cellar. In the process, we may ﬁnd out that the data volumes are not yet suﬃcient or that other data is needed. Together, we deﬁne how the data gap can be ﬁlled.

To ensure that the newly created overview is maintained long term, we take care of data models, databases and documentation. The extremely important topic of data quality management is also part of this step. Because as the saying goes: Sh*t in, sh*t out. Faulty data or data gaps shake conﬁdence in the numbers – often permanently.

After the cleanup, the fun begins. The data is brought to life. I create clear reports, automated processes and breathe life into your data. The result is a data world that everyone loves to use, that inspires and makes daily work easier.

If pure data visualization is still not enough, statistical methods can be used to develop insights that were not apparent at ﬁrst glance. This sometimes feels like magic. Negatively, it could also be called a black box. Therefore, it is important to always add just a pinch.

The better data world has been finally created and the day-to-day work with the analysis results begins. At this point, things often become critical. Unmaintained databases, processes that go nowhere or implausible reports need to be avoided because – rightly! – no one wants to use that. Trust disappears and, in the worst case, everything goes back into the data cellar.

To make sure this doesn't happen, ask me to accompany you and together we'll follow the motto "Your data deserves a better place." In the long run!
